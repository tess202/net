Novak Djokovic admits \u201chistory is hovering\u201d over him as he takes on Casper Ruud in the French Open final on Sunday with a men\u2019s record 23rd Grand Slam title tantalisingly close.<\/p>\n

The 36-year-old Serb can set numerous landmarks in what will be his seventh championship match at Roland Garros.<\/p>\n

As well as breaking the 22-Slam tie he shares with Rafael Nadal, he can become both the tournament\u2019s oldest champion and the only man to win all four majors on at least three occasions.<\/p>\n

In the women\u2019s game, only Margaret Court, Steffi Graf and Serena Williams can make a similar boast.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe only thing I can say is that I\u2019m very focused. History is always something that\u2019s hovering over me,\u201d said Djokovic, the 2016 and 2021 winner in Paris and now the oldest finalist in 93 years.<\/p>\n

Djokovic, playing his 34th Grand Slam final, can also return to the world number one ranking with victory over Ruud, replacing Carlos Alcaraz, the man he defeated in the semi-finals.<\/p>\n

Victory on Sunday would place him once again halfway to an historic calendar Grand Slam, a sweep of all four majors in the same year which has only ever been achieved by two men.<\/p>\n

Rod Laver accomplished it twice, the last coming in 1969.<\/p>\n

Djokovic, however, refuses to contemplate such dominance, wary of how the pressure doomed his last bid to pocket all four Slams in the same year when he lost the 2021 US Open final to Daniil Medvedev.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m not thinking about a calendar Slam. I\u2019m thinking really just to win another Grand Slam title here on Sunday, and I\u2019m so close. I know it. I know the feeling,\u201d said Djokovic.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n
\n

\u2018Talk about history\u2019<\/h2>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n
<\/p>\n
\n
\n

\u201cExperience is on my side, but does it win matches? I don\u2019t think so. I have to be prepared for another long battle, and after the final, if I win, let\u2019s talk about history.\u201d<\/p>\n

Ruud was runner-up to Rafael Nadal in the 2022 final before also losing the championship match at the US Open to Alcaraz.<\/p>\n

The 24-year-old Norwegian has never defeated Djokovic in four meetings and has yet to win a set.<\/p>\n

If that wasn\u2019t intimidating enough, just the presence of Djokovic on the other side of the net presents another challenge.<\/p>\n

\n
\n
<\/p>\n
\n
\n

Alcaraz admitted that the tension he felt in facing the Serb for the first time in a Grand Slam match caused the body cramps which torpedoed his hopes in the semi-final.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n

\n
\n
<\/p>\n
\n
\n

\u201cNovak is a legend of our sport,\u201d said Alcaraz. \u201cIf someone says that he goes onto the court with no nerves playing against Novak, he lies.<\/p>\n

\u201cOf course playing a semi-final of a Grand Slam, you have a lot of nerves, but even more facing Novak. That\u2019s the truth.\u201d<\/p>\n

As Djokovic eventually cruised to victory in Friday\u2019s semi-final, winning 10 of the last 11 games against an ailing Alcaraz, he was booed for fist-pumping as victory closed in.<\/p>\n

However, he is so used to being the pantomime villain of the sport, that it\u2019s become an inspiration.<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t mind. It\u2019s not the first; probably not the last. I\u2019ll just keep winning,\u201d he warned.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n

\n
\n
<\/p>\n
\n

\u2018Novak only human\u2019<\/h2>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n
<\/p>\n
\n
\n

Despite Ruud\u2019s desperately poor record against Djokovic, he can point to his prowess on clay.<\/p>\n

Since 2020, he has the most wins on the surface with 87, most finals with 12 and nine titles.<\/p>\n

On Sunday, the world number four will attempt to make sure that all the pressure sits squarely on the shoulders of Djokovic.<\/p>\n

\u201cI think it\u2019s just a matter of not thinking like, I \u2018need\u2019 to win this match. This is a word that I try to avoid,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Alexander Zverev, defeated in straight sets by Ruud in the semi-finals, believes that Djokovic might crack under the weight of expectation.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf I would have to bet money, maybe I wouldn\u2019t bet on Casper too much. Does he have chances? Yes, he does,\u201d said Zverev after a third successive semi-final loss in Paris.<\/p>\n

\u201cBut I think it couldn\u2019t be better for Casper. Novak is one of the best players in the world, that\u2019s for sure, but when you\u2019re on the brink of history I think that adds a little bit of pressure.<\/p>\n

\u201cYou remember the US Open final he had with Medvedev? The pressure, you know, we are all human. Novak is human.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
